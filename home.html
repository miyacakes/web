Taylor Alison Swift is an American singer-songwriter. She is known for narrative songs about her personal life, which have received widespread media coverage. Born and raised in Reading, Pennsylvania, Swift moved to Nashville, Tennessee, at the age of 14 to pursue a career in country music.

Swift's third studio album, Speak Now (2010), won two Grammy Awards, including Album of the Year, and was followed by Red (2012), 1989 (2014), Reputation (2017), Lover (2019), and Folklore (2020).
